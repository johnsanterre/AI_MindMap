! Rotary Position Embeddings
<img src="assets/rope.svg" alt="RoPE diagram showing two token vectors rotated by angles proportional to their positions, with the dot product depending only on their relative position" style="max-width:100%;margin:1em 0;">

Imagine two clock hands — their relative angle tells you the time difference regardless of what time they both started. Rotary position embeddings encode position by literally rotating token vectors: the angle of the query at position m and the angle of the key at position n encode not just where each token is, but how far apart they are.

!! The Core Insight

''Su et al. 2021 (RoPE)'' proposed encoding position m by rotating the query and key vectors: $q_m = R_m q$ and $k_n = R_n k$. The dot product becomes $q_m \cdot k_n = q^\top R_m^\top R_n k = q^\top R_{n-m} k$, which depends only on the ''relative position'' $(n-m)$, not on absolute positions. This gives transformers the ability to generalize to sequence lengths not seen during training. RoPE is now the dominant position encoding in modern LLMs — used in LLaMA, GPT-NeoX, PaLM, Mistral, and Gemma, among others.

!! The Rotation Mechanism

For a $d$-dimensional vector, RoPE applies independent 2D rotations to each consecutive pair of dimensions:

$$R_m = \begin{pmatrix} \cos m\theta_1 & -\sin m\theta_1 & & & \\ \sin m\theta_1 & \cos m\theta_1 & & & \\ & & \ddots & & \\ & & & \cos m\theta_{d/2} & -\sin m\theta_{d/2} \\ & & & \sin m\theta_{d/2} & \cos m\theta_{d/2} \end{pmatrix}$$

where $\theta_i = 10000^{-2(i-1)/d}$ — the same geometric base as sinusoidal PE. Each dimension pair rotates at a different frequency: low-index pairs rotate fast (capturing local, short-range patterns), high-index pairs rotate slowly (capturing global, long-range structure). The full rotation is thus a ''block-diagonal'' matrix requiring no dense matrix multiplication — only elementwise operations.

!! What the Math Is Actually Doing

The key property is:

$$\langle R_m q,\; R_n k \rangle = \langle q,\; R_{n-m} k \rangle$$

Attention scores are therefore a function of relative position alone. Compare to alternatives:

- ''Sinusoidal PE'' (Vaswani 2017): absolute position, added to embeddings, does not generalize beyond training length.
- ''Learned absolute PE'': absolute, fixed vocabulary of positions, no generalization.
- ''ALiBi'' (Press et al. 2022): adds a linear penalty to attention logits proportional to distance; relative, extrapolates well but operates differently from RoPE.
- ''RoPE'': multiplicative rotation applied to query/key vectors; relative, no extra parameters, smooth extrapolation with context-extension tricks.

''Extrapolation:'' vanilla RoPE degrades at roughly 2× training context. //YaRN// (Peng et al. 2023), //NTK-aware scaling// (change base to $b' = b \cdot (L'/L)^{d/(d-2)}$), and //LongRoPE// (Xiong et al. 2023) extend usable context to 128K+ tokens without retraining from scratch.

!! RoPE vs. Alternatives

|!Method|!Position type|!Generalizes?|!Parameters?|
|Sinusoidal PE|Absolute|Poor|None|
|Learned PE|Absolute|None|Yes|
|ALiBi|Relative (additive)|Good|None|
|RoPE|Relative (rotational)|Good (+ tricks)|None|

RoPE won in practice because it is: (1) relative and parameter-free, (2) efficient — implemented as elementwise complex multiplication, (3) compatible with Flash Attention, and (4) extensible to longer contexts via base rescaling.

!! When RoPE Breaks Down

''Extrapolation without tricks:'' vanilla RoPE degrades sharply beyond 2× training context length. The rotation angles for positions outside the training range are never seen during training, causing attention patterns to collapse.

''Very long contexts:'' even with NTK-aware or YaRN scaling, attention mechanisms can struggle to maintain meaningful dependencies across extremely long spans. The quadratic cost of attention compounds the problem.

''Positional aliasing at extreme lengths:'' at very long contexts, different positions may accumulate near-identical rotation angles in some dimension pairs (due to the periodic nature of sine/cosine), causing the model to confuse positions that are far apart.

!! Technical Details

- ''Original paper:'' Su et al. (2021), "RoFormer: Enhanced Transformer with Rotary Position Embedding."
- ''Base frequency:'' $\theta_i = 10000^{-2(i-1)/d}$; extended to 500,000 in LLaMA-3.
- ''Complex number form:'' represent each 2D pair as a complex number: $q = q_r + i\,q_i$; rotation by $m\theta$ is multiplication by $e^{im\theta}$. This is the standard efficient implementation.
- ''NTK-aware scaling:'' $b' = b \cdot (L'/L)^{d/(d-2)}$ rescales the base so that high-frequency dimensions stay within trained range at extended lengths.
- ''YaRN:'' Peng et al. (2023); interpolates different frequency bands differently, preserving short-range precision while extending long-range coverage.
- ''LongRoPE:'' Xiong et al. (2023); fine-tunes on long contexts with a modified RoPE schedule.
- ''Flash Attention compatibility:'' RoPE is applied to Q and K before the attention kernel, so it is fully compatible with Flash Attention 1 and 2.
- Adopted by: LLaMA (1, 2, 3), GPT-NeoX, PaLM, Mistral, Gemma, Phi-3, Qwen, Falcon, and most open-source LLMs post-2022.
